About Me

Hey, I’m Ketan Thakur — a second-year Computer Science student at VIT-AP, currently pursuing a 5-year Integrated B.Tech + M.Tech degree. But more than what I’m studying, this journey is about who I’m becoming — and the road to this point has been anything but straight.

I didn’t grow up in a house filled with gadgets or coding conversations. Tech wasn’t my starting line. I come from a place where discipline meant waking up before sunrise, physical strength was valued above all, and dreams often wore a soldier's uniform. For the longest time, my biggest goal was to get into the NDA. That’s what I trained for — physically, mentally, emotionally. And when that dream didn’t work out, it hit hard. But failure, I’ve learned, doesn’t mean the end — sometimes it just redirects you to something you never saw coming.

I found myself stepping into the world of technology, not with excitement at first, but with uncertainty. I had no idea what I was doing. But I decided to treat it the same way I treated my NDA prep — with discipline, consistency, and heart. I began exploring Java, Python, DSA, and problem-solving — brick by brick. There were times I felt behind, out of place, or lost. But I kept showing up. And now, slowly, I’m building not just knowledge, but confidence — in my work, in my journey, in myself.

Beyond the classroom and the code, fitness remains a big part of who I am. It’s not just about building muscle — it’s about showing up for myself, even on the days when I don’t feel like it. That gym grind, the soreness, the discipline — it reminds me of where I started and where I still want to go. And yeah — nothing feels quite like walking on a broken ankle, not because it’s smart, but because it reminds me I’ve still got that fight in me. That voice inside that says, “Keep going.”

And then there’s the part of me that writes. Poetry. Shayari. Stories. Emotions I can’t say out loud but spill perfectly in words. My Instagram page @kt4nwrites is my quiet corner of the world where I post pieces of my heart. Sometimes people read them and say, “I felt that.” And honestly, that means more to me than likes or followers ever could.

I don’t claim to have it all figured out. I’m not the top coder in my batch or the fittest guy in the gym. But I try. Every single day. I try to be a little better, a little wiser, a little more patient with myself and the process. I believe in becoming good at many things — not chasing perfection, but progress.

I’ve always felt like I live in two worlds — one where I’m chasing tech dreams, coding problems, and building a future… and another where I’m just a guy from Himachal, who once dreamt of the NDA, still hits the gym every evening, and writes poetry late into the night. And I guess I’m learning that both versions of me are real — and valid.

So if you’re here, reading this, maybe we’ve got something in common. Or maybe you’re just curious. Either way — I’m glad you’re here. I’m open to new projects, collaborations, conversations, or just honest connections — whether it’s about tech, fitness, poetry, or figuring out life one day at a time.

Let’s build something — or at least have a real talk.
